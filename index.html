It's definitely strange to see the tyrant this low, but here on my list,its at the very bottom

Tyrannosaurus was a north american theropod dinosaur, this 12 tonne, 13 meter beast was the most massive land predator to ever exist, it was discovered in 1902, this coelurosaur lived in the hell creek formation, this formation stretches over portions of Montana, North Dakota, South Dakota, and Wyoming, it lived in Maastrichtian age of the late Cretaceous, the very end of the age of the dinosaurs. Tyrannosaurus was an adept hunter,it had the strongest bite of anything on land, vision around 6.5 times better than an eagles and it also had smell comparable to a turkey vulture and could here as well as a fennec fox. it also had intelligence akin to that of a chimpanzee. Torvosaurus tanneri

For this one, we will go to the most famous era of the Mesozoic, The Jurassic!

Torvosaurus is a species of theropod dinosaur from the Late Jurassic Morrison Formation of North America. It is known from the Carnegie Quarry at Dinosaur National Monument from a single bone, the humerus. This upper arm bone is more robust and straighter than its more common contemporary, Allosaurus. Torvosaurus was likely the largest theropod in the Morrison Formation. It also had a much stronger bite than its contemperaries, this was mainly due to competiton, as torvosaurus ost likely hunted different prey and might have even eaten diffrent parts of a carcass

Carnotaurus sasteri

This animal comes from a family known as "walking sausages" by the paleo community, and this one is a oddball in a family of oddbals!

Carnotaurus was a predatory dinosaur. It lived 70 million years ago on the Cretaceous period. It was a large theropod which lived in South America. The skeleton, found in 1984, was uncovered in Argentina. Carnotaurus was a lightly built, bipedal predator, 8m in length and weighing 2 metric tons. Carnotaurus was highly specialized and distinctive. It had horns above the eyes, a feature not seen in other carnivorous dinosaurs, and a very deep skull sitting on a muscular neck. Also, it had small, vestigial forelimbs, smaller even than those of Tyrannosaurus. The distinctive horns and muscular neck may have been used in fighting rivals of its own species. Rivals may have fought with quick head blows, by slow pushes with the upper sides of their skulls, or by ramming each other head-on, using their horns as shock Carnotaurus was well adapted for running and was possibly one of the fastest large theropods, though it could not make sharp turns.

Styracosaurus albertensis

Finally, an herbivore, this may be the second most famous member of its family, after triceratops of course

Like i mentioned earlier, this dino is in the same family as triceratops, and you can definatley see the similarities, but you can also spot a lot of differnces! Styracosaurus was a relatively large dinosaur, reaching lengths of 5–5.5 metres and weighing about 1.8–2.7 tons. Styracosaurus possessed four short legs and a bulky body. Its tail was rather short. The skull had a beak and shearing cheek teeth arranged in continuous dental batteries, suggesting that the animal sliced up plants. Like other ceratopsians, this dinosaur may have been a herd animal, travelling in large groups.

Pachyrhinosaurus lakustai

Ah,another herbivore, this one definitely stands out, even in its family

Pachyrhinosaurus is certainly a strage ceratopsian, with a nasal boss instead of a horn. Three species have been found to belong to this genus, P.lakusta,P.canadensis(largest) and P.perotorum(smallest). P.lakustai is my favourite because of the row of spikes on its frill. This centrosaur lived in the northernmost parts of North America, some say that it had a wooly coat for warmth, this has been called into question in resent times, but hey, in my opion it looks much better! The largest Pachyrhinosaurus were 8 metres (26 ft) long. It weighed about four tons. They were herbivorous and possessed strong cheek teeth to help them chew tough, fibrous plants. Instead of horns, their skulls bore massive, flattened bosses; a large boss over the nose and a smaller one over the eyes. A prominent pair of horns grew from the frill and extended upwards, and the skull also bore several smaller horns.

Ceratosaurus dentisulcatus

We are back in the Jurassic, and this is my favourite dinosaur from that entire era

Ceratosaurus dentisulcatus is one of the three species belonging to the genus Ceratosaurus, the other two being Ceratosaurus nasicornis and Ceratosaurus magnicornis, dentisulcatus is the largest out the the three. The Ceratosaurus lived in both western North America, but also parts of Africa it shared it's habitat with larger carnivores such as the allosaurus and torvosaurus, it may have avoided compeition by hunting different prey or eating different parts of a carcass, it's teeth were incredibly long for a dinosaur it's size, suggesting it ate mainly the internal organs of its prey, unlike allosaurus that would mainly eat the flesh. The Ceratosaurus may have been one of the more medium-sized predators, so it may have needed less food than its counterpart predators.
